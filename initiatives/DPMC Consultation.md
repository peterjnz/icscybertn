# NZ ICS Cyber Technical Network (TN) Initiative
## Proposed Plan: TN Engagement on DPMC Critical Infrastructure Cyber Consultation

## Purpose

The NZ ICS Cyber Technical Network (ICS Cyber TN)—currently comprising 423 practitioners working across industrial control systems (ICS) and operational technology (OT) cybersecurity in New Zealand—has significant interest in the ongoing consultation led by the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet (DPMC) on critical infrastructure protection and cybersecurity obligations.

Given the practical and operational expertise represented within the community (asset owners, operators, service providers, and technology vendors), the ICS Cyber TN intends to facilitate a structured technical discussion forum to enable practitioner input into the consultation process.

This initiative aims to support constructive engagement with government while ensuring that operational technology perspectives are clearly represented in the policy development process.

---

## Proposed ICS Cyber TN Community Engagement

To support informed practitioner input, the ICS Cyber TN proposes to host a community technical forum in early April, following the public consultation briefings conducted by government agencies.

### Forum Objectives

The forum will aim to:

- Provide a technical discussion space for ICS/OT practitioners
- Allow government representatives to present and clarify the proposal
- Identify operational impacts on critical infrastructure operators
- Gather structured feedback from practitioners
- Inform a community submission to the consultation

---

## Proposed Forum Structure

Timing: Early April 2026  
Audience: Members of the NZ ICS Cyber Technical Network (423 practitioners)  
Format: Virtual technical forum

### Presenters (proposed)

- Representatives from:
  - Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet (DPMC)
  - National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC)
  - NZ ICS Cyber TN committee

### Facilitation

Forum chaired by Peter Jackson or members of the ICS Cyber TN committee (5 members).

### NOMINAL Discussion Topics

- Scope of CI definitions  
- Operational feasibility of proposed measures  
- Impact on ICS / OT environments  
- Information sharing  
- Incident reporting considerations  
- Risk management expectations  
- Implications of ministerial direction powers

---

## Community Feedback Collection

To ensure inclusive participation, feedback will be gathered before, during, and after the forum.

### Feedback Mechanism

A Google Form hosted by ICS Cyber TN will collect community input.

Characteristics:

- Anonymous responses  
- Open to all ICS Cyber TN members  
- Combination of:
  - Structured questions aligned to consultation measures  
  - Open comment fields

### NZ ICS Cyber TN Member Feedback Phases

| Phase | Activity |
|------|------|
| Pre-Forum | Members submit initial views on the consultation |
| During Forum | Discussion of themes emerging from responses |
| Post-Forum | Additional feedback and refinement of community views |

---

## Output

The feedback will be lightly analyzed by the ICS Cyber TN committee to identify common themes and practitioner perspectives.

This analysis is intended to inform a formal ICS Cyber TN community submission to the consultation led by the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet.

The intent is not to represent a single consensus view, but rather to ensure that the practical realities of securing industrial control systems and operational technology environments are reflected in the consultation process.

## Context

The consultation on critical infrastructure protection proposes several measures affecting entities operating critical infrastructure across New Zealand.

Reference:  
https://www.dpmc.govt.nz/our-programmes/national-security/critical-infrastructure

### Section 1 – Definition of Critical Infrastructure

The consultation proposes definitions for in-scope critical infrastructure (CI) entities. This scope is expected to include organizations such as Genesis Energy and other operators of essential services.

### Section 2 – Cybersecurity Measures for Critical Infrastructure

Six proposed measures relating to cybersecurity preparedness and information sharing:

1. Measure 1 – Government information collection powers  
   Allow government to collect specific operational and cybersecurity information from CI entities.

2. Measure 2 – Voluntary information sharing framework  
   Establish an enhanced voluntary information exchange framework (potential successor to CSSIE).

3. Measure 3 – Mandatory information sharing between CI entities  
   Require CI operators to share certain cyber-relevant information with peer organizations.

4. Measure 4 – Mandatory cyber incident reporting  
   Introduce requirements for CI entities to report cybersecurity incidents.

5. Measure 5 – Cyber risk management programs  
   Require CI entities to maintain formal cyber risk management frameworks.

6. Measure 6 – Ministerial direction powers  
   Allow the responsible Minister to direct a CI entity to take (or refrain from taking) specific actions in response to a cyber risk or incident.

### Section 3 – Compliance Framework

The consultation proposes a staged compliance regime, which may ultimately introduce regulatory enforcement and financial penalties.
